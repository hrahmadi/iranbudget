## KEY OBSERVATIONS

### 1. **Oil Revenue Decline**
- Oil/gas revenue dropped 31.8% from 1399 to 1400
- Likely due to sanctions impact and COVID-19 effects
- Budget law set ceiling of 1,992,720 billion rials, but actual was only 721,092

### 2. **Tax Revenue Growth**
- Tax revenue increased 12.5% year-over-year
- Government compensating for oil revenue loss with tax collection
- All tax categories grew at similar ~12% rate

### 3. **Budget Deficit Emergence**
- 1399: Balanced budget (revenues = expenses)
- 1400: Deficit of 1,108,098 billion rials
- Expenses grew 47.5% while revenues grew only 24.8%

### 4. **Expenditure Growth**
- Total spending increased 47.5% year-over-year
- Capital expenditure grew 14.2%
- Most expenditure classification unclear from CSV data

### 5. **Subsidy System**
- Subsidy spending relatively stable (+12.4%)
- Subsidy revenues (from various sources) also grew 12.4%
- Suggests continuation of subsidy targeting reform
